No, you lunatic! Potatoes have to be cooked first! Below I'll include some suggestions for how to do just that!

For Yukon golds

  1. Wash your selected tatoes
  2. Put them in a pot
  3. Fill the pot with cold, salted water until the taters are submerged
  4. Place on stove
  5. Bring water slowly to a boil
  6. Once the potatoes are soft enough to be easily pierced with a fork take them off the heat
  7. Drain the water and grab butter and sour cream from the fridge
  8. Mash the potatoes with your preferred implement and add in lots of butter and sour cream (even a ranch packet if you're feeling saucey)
  9. Mash into a creamy consistency and taste, add salt and pepper to your liking
  10. Eat the entire bowl in one sitting and then gently slip into a coma

Russet or Golds

  1. Wash Taters
  2. Trim the taters into a long but even cube shape, kinda like a stick of butter
  3. Get out your mandolin
  4. Mandolin the cubes into thin domino shapes
  5. Soak the dominoes in cold water for at least 30mins
  6. Drain the water and dry the dominoes as best you can
  7. Lay the dominoes on a greased baking tray slightly overlapping in a spiral, working from the outside in
  8. Place several dollops of clarified butter or ghee around the tops of the dominoes
  9. Into the oven at ~425 degrees Farenheit
  10. Check them at 10 mins
  11. Remove them once they are golden brown and sprinkle them with salt
  12. Tip the tray up and dump them in your mouth/on your face
  13. Don't tell any of your roommates who spilled taters everywhere
  14. Move out of your apt and assume a new identity
